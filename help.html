Quick Start

Start Here

Get your first route planned in under two minutes:

1
Get a free Groq API key

Visit console.groq.com, create a free account, and generate an API key. It takes about 60 seconds. See the full guide below.

2
Enter your API key

Paste it into the GROQ API KEY field at the top. The status will turn green — the bar then auto-collapses to save space.

3
Set your start and end

Enter a starting town in START and your destination in END. Add via towns if you want to route through specific places.

4
Choose your POIs and hit Plan

Tick the points of interest you want (free camps, dump points, lookouts, etc.) then click Plan My Route.

5
Open each leg in Google Maps

Expand any day card and tap ↗ Drive A → B to open that leg in Google Maps. On mobile it launches the Maps app directly.

💡
Try a Quick Start route from the sidebar — Classic Big Lap, Kimberley Explorer, Outback Queensland, Great Ocean Road, or Red Centre. These pre-fill all waypoints so you can see the app in action immediately.

Getting Your Free Groq API Key

Required

This app uses Groq to power the AI route planning. Groq offers a generous free tier — more than enough for personal RV trip planning. No credit card required.

✅
Groq free tier includes thousands of AI requests per day at no cost. You will not be charged for normal trip planning usage.
1
Go to the Groq Console

Open console.groq.com in your browser.

2
Create a free account

Click Sign Up. You can sign up with Google, GitHub, or an email address. No credit card is needed.

3
Go to API Keys

Once logged in, click API Keys in the left sidebar (or go to console.groq.com/keys).

4
Create a new key

Click Create API Key. Give it a name like RV Route Planner. Click Submit.

5
Copy your key

The key appears once — copy it immediately. It starts with gsk_ and is about 56 characters long.

6
Paste it into the app

Return to the Route Planner, paste into the GROQ API KEY field. The status badge turns green and the bar collapses automatically. Your key is saved locally in your browser for next time.

⚠️
Keep your key private. Your key is stored only in your browser's local storage — it is never sent to anyone except Groq's servers when you plan a route. Do not share your key or commit it to a public repository.
🔗 console.groq.com/keys

Planning a Route

The left panel contains all your trip settings. Fill these in before hitting Plan My Route.

🔄
Trip Type

Big Lap (full circumnavigation), Regional Loop, Coastal Run, or Custom. Selecting a type pre-fills sensible defaults.

📍
Route Waypoints

Set your start and end town. Add via towns to route through specific places along the way.

📅
Trip Duration

From a weekend to a full year. Affects how the AI breaks up legs and paces the itinerary.

🚐
Rig Type & Length

Caravan, motorhome, campervan, ute canopy, 5th wheeler, or pop-top. Length affects road suitability advice.

📝
Preferences & Notes

Free text — tell the AI anything: travelling with dogs, prefer free camps, avoid steep grades, solar setup, need dump points every 3 days.

⚡
Quick Start Routes

Pre-built routes with all waypoints filled in. Great for inspiration or testing the app.

💡
The Preferences field is powerful. The AI reads everything you write there. Mention your dog, your solar capacity, whether you need powered sites, fuel range concerns — anything that affects your travel style.

Routing Via Specific Towns

Use via towns to make the AI route through specific places. For example, to go Gladstone → Clermont → Townsville:

1
Set START to Gladstone QLD

Type the full town name including state for best results.

2
Click + Add Via Town

A new row appears between START and END. Type Clermont QLD.

3
Set END to Townsville QLD

The app builds the route: Gladstone → Clermont → Townsville.

4
Plan My Route

The AI creates separate legs for each segment and Google Maps links open each leg as a clean A→B drive.

ℹ️
You can add up to 8 via towns between start and end (10 waypoints total). The + Add Via Town button disappears when you reach the limit. Remove a via town with its × button.
⚠️
Always include the state abbreviation — e.g. Clermont QLD not just Clermont — so Google Maps resolves to the correct Australian location.

Vehicle Import & Fuel Calculator

Optional

Once a route is planned, the app can estimate your total fuel cost in AUD for the trip, broken down by leg.

ℹ️
Import from LogMyJourney AU reads your vehicle data (consumption, tank size, fuel type) directly from the same browser if you use the LogMyJourney AU trip logger app. No typing required.
1
Import or enter manually

Click ⬇ Import from LogMyJourney to auto-fill from your trip log data, or click ✏️ Enter Manually to type your consumption (L/100km), tank size, and fuel type.

2
Set the fuel price

A fuel price bar appears once your vehicle is set. Enter the current price per litre in AUD (e.g. $2.15). This updates all estimates instantly.

3
Read your estimates

A fuel summary card shows total distance, total litres, total cost (AUD), number of fill-ups, and cost per km. Each day card also shows the leg cost as a small badge.

💡
If LogMyJourney detects you have a caravan or towing setup, it automatically applies a +35% fuel penalty to account for towing drag. This is shown clearly on the vehicle pill. You can override it by entering values manually.

Google Maps Links

Every leg and POI has a Google Maps link. These are designed to open cleanly — no encoded POI addresses embedded in route URLs.

↗️
Drive A → B

Opens Google Maps driving directions for just that day's leg — clean start-to-end, no waypoints. Tap on mobile to launch the Maps app.

🗺️
Open Full Route

Opens the entire trip in Google Maps, with your via towns threaded as actual waypoints. Limited to 8 waypoints (Google's free limit).

📍
📍 Maps (per POI)

Opens a Google Maps search for that specific place — gives you a clean map pin, photos, hours, and reviews.

▣
QR Code

Generates a scannable QR code for each leg's directions URL. Scan with your phone camera while at camp to jump straight into Maps navigation.

⚠️
Google Maps free URL directions support a maximum of 8 waypoints. For Big Lap routes with many via towns, the full route link uses the first 8. All individual day card links are always clean A→B with no limit.
▣

QR Codes

Handy on the Road

QR codes let you plan on a laptop then instantly navigate on your phone — no typing, no copying URLs.

1
Plan your route on a desktop or laptop

The full planning interface works best on a larger screen.

2
Expand a day card and click ▣ QR Code

A QR modal appears with the Google Maps directions URL encoded as a QR code.

3
Scan with your phone camera

Point your phone camera at the QR code. Tap the banner that appears to open directly in Google Maps navigation.

4
Or save the QR as an image

Click ⬇ Save QR to download a PNG. Print it, add it to a day sheet, or send it to your travel companion.

AI Chat

The AI Chat tab gives you a conversation with the AI after a route is planned. The AI has full context of your itinerary and can help you refine it.

Things to ask:

?
Fuel gap questions

"Is there enough fuel between Camooweal and Tennant Creek? What's the distance and are there any roadhouses?"

?
Free camp details

"Tell me more about free camping options near Longreach — are dogs allowed?"

?
Seasonal advice

"We're leaving in April — should we do the Kimberley clockwise or counter-clockwise to beat the crowds?"

?
Road conditions

"Is the Gibb River Road suitable for a 7.5m caravan in June?"

💡
Plan a route first before using chat — the AI uses your itinerary as context for more accurate answers. If you ask about a road or camp not in your plan, the AI will still do its best with general knowledge.
⚠️
The AI uses general knowledge trained to mid-2025. Road conditions, fuel prices, and campground availability change — always verify critical information with WikiCamps, Campermate, or the relevant state road authority before heading out.

Exporting Your Route

Three export options are available in the bottom toolbar:

📄
Markdown

Downloads a .md file with your full itinerary. Open in any text editor, Obsidian, Notion, or paste into a Word doc. Filename includes today's date in DD-MM-YYYY format.

⚙️
JSON

Downloads the raw structured route data. Useful for importing into other apps or future LogMyJourney integration.

📋
Copy Text

Copies the full itinerary as plain text to your clipboard. Paste directly into a message, email, or notes app.

▣
QR Codes (per leg)

Save individual leg QR codes as PNG images from each day card's QR modal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this cost money?
The app itself is free. Groq's free tier includes thousands of AI requests per day — more than enough for personal trip planning. You only need a Groq account (free, no credit card required).
Is my API key safe?
Your key is stored only in your browser's local storage (rv_groq_key). It is sent directly to Groq's servers when you plan a route and nowhere else. The app has no server of its own. Never share your key publicly.
The Google Maps link looks weird or very long — why?
This happens if POI names (like "Puma Energy Clermont" or "Cape Hillsborough National Park") get included as route waypoints — Google Maps encodes them as full addresses with GPS coordinates. The app now uses POI names only as individual search links, not as waypoints, so route links should be clean. If you see a long URL, try re-planning the route.
Why does the AI sometimes suggest inaccurate places?
The AI draws on general knowledge and may occasionally suggest camps, roadhouses, or services that have closed or changed. Always verify critical stops — especially fuel and water — with WikiCamps, Campermate, or the relevant state road authority before heading out remote.
Can I import my vehicle from LogMyJourney?
Yes — click Import from LogMyJourney in the vehicle bar. It reads triplog_settings from your browser's local storage. This only works if you have used LogMyJourney AU in the same browser on the same device.
The AI Chat isn't responding — what's wrong?
Check that your API key is entered and the status badge shows green. Plan a route first — the chat works best with a route loaded. If you see an error message in the chat bubble, it will tell you what went wrong (e.g. invalid key, rate limit exceeded).
Can I use this on my phone while travelling?
Yes — the layout is responsive and works on mobile. Planning is easier on a larger screen, but you can use the AI chat and open Maps links on mobile. QR codes are especially useful: plan on the laptop at camp, then scan with your phone to navigate each day.
Does this work offline?
No — AI route planning requires an internet connection to reach Groq's servers. Once a route is planned you can export it as Markdown or JSON to keep an offline copy, or save QR codes as images before heading somewhere remote.
